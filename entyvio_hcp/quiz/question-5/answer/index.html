VEDOIBD: study design

This study was an observational, RWE, mITT study based on prospectively collected data from the VEDOIBD registry

This RWE study enrolled patients who were included in the VEDOIBD registry from 45 IBD-experienced centers across Germany.

The study aimed to compare the effectiveness of vedolizumab versus anti-TNF in biologic-naïve patients with CD.

Switchers of a drug were considered as treatment failures in the mITT analysis, whereas switchers were excluded in the per protocol analysis. To reduce selection bias in the estimation of treatment effects, the IPTW PS was implemented.

Limitations: This study was not a randomized controlled clinical study. Some of the results suggesting that vedolizumab was more effective than anti-TNF were significant only in the subgroup analysis comparing vedolizumab to ADA and IFX. Clinical assessments (eg, use of HBI vs CDAI) were less detailed, and the study conduct was less stringent than would have been the case with a randomized controlled trial. Continuous endoscopic data were not available because endoscopies were carried out solely at the discretion of the treating physician, making a reliable evaluation of these data impossible. Observational studies are subject to selection bias and confounding, but these were accounted for using a propensity score matching approach. This study included adult patients only.

* Defined as HBI score ≤ 4.

EVOLVE Expansion: Study design
This multicenter, observational, retrospective cohort study included patients who were treated at 31 sites in Australia, Belgium, and Switzerland.

The study evaluated the real-world clinical effectiveness and safety of vedolizumab and UST as first-line biologic treatments for patients with CD.

Inverse probability of treatment weighting was used to balance demographic and clinical characteristics across treatment groups.

Limitation: This study was not a randomized controlled clinical study.
